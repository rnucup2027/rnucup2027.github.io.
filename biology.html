The Objectives

Overall:

  1. To equip students with a comprehensive understanding of the fundamental principles of biology, fostering critical thinking skills, scientific curiosity, and a deep appreciation for the living world.
  2. Obtain a teacher's recommendation on your academic potential and character.
  3. Share your entrance exam scores and middle school transcripts.

Knowledge:

  1. Students will be able to define and explain key biological concepts, principles, and processes.
  2. Students will be able to identify and classify different levels of biological organization (e.g., cells, tissues, organs, organisms, populations, ecosystems).
  3. Students will be able to explain the principles of cellular respiration, photosynthesis, and other metabolic processes.
  4. Students will be able to describe the process of evolution and its role in shaping the diversity of life.

Comprehension:

  1. Students will be able to analyze and interpret scientific data (e.g., graphs, charts, tables).
  2. Students will be able to form and test hypotheses using the scientific method.
  3. Students will be able to draw conclusions from scientific experiments and observations.
  4. Students will be able to evaluate the validity of scientific claims and information.

Application:

  1. Students will be able to apply their biological knowledge to solve real-world problems (e.g., environmental issues, health concerns).
  2. Students will be able to design and conduct their own scientific investigations.
  3. Students will be able to communicate scientific findings effectively in written and oral formats.
  4. Students will be able to critically evaluate the ethical implications of biological research and technology.

Synthesis:

  1. Students will be able to design and conduct original research that contributes to the field of biology.
