Our Aims

Overall:

  1. Students will be able to analyze and interpret complex texts across various genres (literature, non-fiction, poetry, etc.).
  2. Students will be able to write clear, concise, and grammatically correct prose in various formats (essays, reports, research papers, etc.).
  3. They will be able to adapt their writing style to different audiences and purposes.

Knowledge:

  1. Students will be able to deliver clear and engaging presentations on various topics.
  2. They will be able to participate effectively in discussions and group activities.
  3. They will be able to articulate their ideas confidently and persuasively in both formal and informal settings.
  4. They will be able to properly cite sources and avoid plagiarism.

Comprehension:

  1. Students will be exposed to diverse literary and non-literary texts from various cultures and historical periods.
  2. They will be able to analyze and appreciate different cultural perspectives and expressions.
  3. They will be able to engage in critical discussions about issues of diversity, equity, and inclusion.

Application:

  1. Students will be able to effectively research a topic, identify credible sources, and evaluate information critically.
  2. They will be able to synthesize information from multiple sources to form their own arguments and conclusions.
  3. Students will hone their written and oral communication skills, including clarity, conciseness, and appropriate language register.

Synthesis:

  1. Students will cultivate an appreciation for the beauty and power of language as a tool for communication and expression.
